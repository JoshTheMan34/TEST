Nintendo DS

2004-2005

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DS-Fat-Blue. 2004.
.en.wikipedia.org (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_DS)

The Nintendo DS was released globally across 2004 and 2005 and was meant to complement the Game Boy Advanced family and GameCube. However, due to the system's features and strong titles, sales, etc, it eventually established itself as the successor to the Game Boy series. The DS was the first Nintendo system to feature the ability to directly interact with other DS consoles over Wi-Fi within a specific range without the need to connect to an already established wireless network. The DS also released with Nintendo's new Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection. The DS was marketed as Nintendo's competating console to Sony's PlayStation Portable. The DS launched in North America on November 21, 2004 for US$149.99 and in various other markets around the world. The DS sold for 154.02 million units across all of its models. The DS's sucess introduced touchscreen controls and wireless online gaming to a wider range of audiences. It introduced a casual and portable gaming market which attracted non-gamers and gamers alike. The DS readied the world for the release of the Iphone in 2007. The desing of the DS was inspired by the Game & Watch line of games such as Donkey Kong and The Legends of Zelda. The lower screen accepted input from a sylus, touch by the user's fingers, and other accessories. The DS lanuched with a number of games, the most recognizable in NA including Super Mario 64 DS, Spider-Man 2, Metroid Prime Hunters: First Hunt, and Madden NFL 2005. The DS led to a lineage of hand held consoles and paved the way for the futrue of Nintendo

Nintendo DS Lite

2006-2007

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DS-Lite-Black-Open. 2011.
.commons.wikimedia.org (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Nintendo-DS-Lite-Black-Open.jpg)

The Nintendo DS Lite is the second generation of the DS line of consoles. It has a slimmer, brighter, and lighterweight design compared to the original Nintendo DS. It was released in Australia, North America, Europe, New Zealand, Singapore, and certain regions in South America, the Middle East, and East Asia. It has shipped a total of 93.86 million units worldwide. The original price of the DS Lite was $130. The DS Lite was considered the first console sold in South Korea by the Nintendo subsidiary, Nintendo of Korea, on January 18, 2007. Jang Dong-gun and Ahn Sung-ki (two popular Korean actors) were hired to help promote the console at its release. The DS Lite measured in at 2.91 in. tall, 5.2 in. wide, and 0.85 in. deep. The top screen is 256x192-pixel resolution and is capable of displaying a total of 262,144 colors. The top and touch screen have the same capability and hardware. However, the touch screen has a transparent analog touch sensor. The DS Lite has A/B/X/Y buttons on the face of the device along with a directional control pad, L/R shoulder buttons, a start and select button, a volume slider, and a power switch. The DS Lite comes with a sylus about 1 cm longer, and 2 mm thicker than the original DS sylus. It boasts stereo speakers and has ports for stereo headphones and an embedded mircrophone for voice recognition. The DS Lite is cross compatible with Game Boy Advance Games as well as original Nintendo DS game. To achieve this, the DS Lite has a DS slot on top of the console and a Game Boy slot on the bottom.

Nintendo DSi / DSi XL

2008-2009

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-DSi-Bl-Open. 2011.
.commons.wikimedia.org (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Nintendo-DSi-Bl-Open.jpg)

The Nintendo DSi is the second generation of the Nintendo DS line of consoles. It was released in Japan on November 1, 2008 and worldwide in April 2009. The larger model named the Nintendo DSi XL released in Japan on November 21, 2009 and worldwide in March 2010. The original launch price of the DSi was $169.99 and $189.99 for the DSi XL. The core design of the DSi is similar to that of the DS Lite, however, the DSi features two digital cameras, supports internal and external storage, and connects to an online store that Nintendo coined the Nintendo DSi Shop. The new functionality was meant to lead the user to personalize their DSi. This was to encourage family members to buy indiviual DSi for each member of the family who wanted one instead of having one DSi per household. The DSi offers exclusive physical media along with DS games that include specific DSi features along with the ability to play original DS games. The only problem is that you cannot play earlier DS games on the DSi that require a GBA slot. The DSi and DSi XL have combined sold a total of 41 million units. Reviews for the DSi were overall positive, however there was some complaints due to the DSi's cameras low resolution and the inability to play games that require the GBA cartridge. The DSi was seen as the pinacle console in the DS family of consoles. It was followed up and succeeded by the new line of DS consoles called the Nintendo 3DS.

Nintendo 3DS / 3DS XL

2011

Source: Evan-Amos. Nintendo-3DS-AquaOpen. 2010.
.en.wikipedia.org (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_3DS)

The Nintendo 3DS is the successor to the Nintendo DS line of consoles. The 3DS was announced in March 2010 and first shown at E3 2010. The 3DS was first releaed in Japan on Febuary 26, 2011 and released worldwide on July 28, 2011. The 3DS released at $249 to a disappointing launch. The significant price led the 3DS to have drastically lower launch sales than expected. Due to this, Nintendo lowered the price to $169. An XL version of the 3DS was also released for $199.99 similarly to the DSi and DSi XL. The primary feature new to the 3DS was the stereoscopic 3D effects on the screen of the console without the use of 3D goggles. The 3DS also featured the new StreetPass and SpotPass tag modes made using the Nintendo Network. Augmented reality was implimented using the 3DS' 3D cameras. A virtual console was also added to the 3DS which allows users to play games originally released on older Nintendo gaming consoles. The Nintendo eShop made its debute on the 3DS. An application called the Miiverese was realeased on the 3DS and acted as a social networking service. The 3DS also had the ability to use applications like an internet browser, Netflix, Hulu Plus, and Youtube. Nintendo Video, a messaging app called Swapnote and Mii Maker are other notable applications available on the 3DS. The hardware of the 3DS was a noticable improvement to the DSi boasting 2 GB of eMMC flash memory compared to the DSi's 256 MB of internal flash memory, a dual-core ARM11 MPCORE based CPU clocked at 268 MHz. The GPU of the 3DS is a PICA200 clocked at 268 MHz. Lastly the 3DS featured the Circle Pad, a round nub-like analog stick and six-axis motion sensor that includes a 3-axis accelerometer and a 3-axis gyroscope.

"new" Nintendo 3DS / 3DS XL

2014-2015

Source: fishpond.comfj. New Nintendo 3DS XL - Black.
.www.fishpond.com.fj (https://www.fishpond.com.fj/Games/New-Nintendo-3DS-XL-Black/0045496781514)

The last and newest generation of the Nintendo DS and 3DS family of consoles is the "new" 3DS and "new" 3DS XL. The "new" 3DS was released in Japan on October 11, 2014 and worldwide through 2014 and September 25, 2015. The original price for the "new" 3DS was $219.99 for the bundle and $199.99 for the "new" 3DS XL.The "new" 3DS improves on the original 3DS in an abundance of ways. The "new" 3DS has upgraded processors, an increased amount of RAM, a C-stick (analog pointing stick) and two additional shoulder triggers which are called ZR and ZL. The C-Stick allows the "new" 3DS to have backwards compatability with games that use features equivalent to the Circle Pad Pro add-on. The "new" 3DS also has face detection to optimize the autostereoscopic 3D display. The "new" 3DS also includes a 4 GB microSD card and NFC built into the system. New design changes such as colored buttons on the face of the console, curved edges, and a different placement of buttons differentiate the "new" 3DS and the original 3DS. The "new" 3DS also includes the ability to read and use Amiibo thanks to the NFC. The "new" 3DS continued to use the same AC adapter as the original DSi, DSi XL, and the 3DS. The "new" 3DS was compatable with previous DSi, DSi XL, and origianal DS and DS Lite games released for each respective console. The "new" 3DS saw the return of the Nintendo eShop as well as StreetPass and SpotPass. The web browser got an update that allowed for support of HTML-5 based video playback. Nintendo discontinued production of the "new" Nintendo 3DS in July 2019 with the arrival of Nintendo's newest console, the Nintendo Switch. However, the "new" 3DS is still supported to this day.
